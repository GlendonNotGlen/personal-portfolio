About Me

Year 2 Undergraduate.

Seeking to join, contribute and grow our cybersecurity ecosystem in Singapore. So that we can collectively strengthen our cyber space.

The SingHealth hack in 2018 which affected millions of Singaporeans, including a close family member, spurred me to enroll in an undergraduate course in Information Security. I have since completed several modules on computer security that covers a broad range of topics. From low-level binary exploits to application-level injection attacks, the ingenuity of these attacks has solidified my decision to have an exciting career in the domain of cybersecurity.

Outside of curriculum, I actively seek out community engagement opportunities. Earlier this year, I conducted a workshop on creating a personal portfolio website for over a hundred participants and volunteered with Autism Resource Centre to teach Python programming to students with Autism. Being a part-time Teaching Assistant at the School of Computing over the past semester has also reaffirmed my passion to teach and nurture others.

Currently, I am an intern at Cyber Security Agency of Singapore, while volunteering at Centre For Computing for Social Good & Philanthropy (CCSGP) to teach an introductory course on Artificial Intelligence to students with Autism. As the current President of Google Developer Student Club (GDSC) NUS, I also lead a team of extremely passionate individuals to use technology as a driving force for good.

In my free time, I enjoy watching documentaries, personal finance videos, or a game of League of Legends. I also play volleyball occasionally!
